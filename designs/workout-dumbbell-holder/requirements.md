# Workout Dumbbell Holder Requirements

## Design Intent

A clip-on cradle that mounts one rubber-coated dumbbell horizontally on a treadmill's rectangular aluminum frame extrusion. Two units are printed (one per dumbbell), but the design is a single symmetric part. The mount portion inserts into the hollow interior of the extrusion (internal plug), and outside the extrusion the part transitions into a forked Y-profile whose two tines form a curved U-saddle for the dumbbell shaft. The near bell acts as the inboard axial stop; the fork tines prevent lateral shaft escape. The dumbbell hangs cantilevered, roughly perpendicular to the extrusion, supported entirely by the holder.

Reference photo: `attachments/2026-05-07-workout-dumbbell-holder-reference.jpg` (orange rubber-coated dumbbell resting horizontally between two rectangular extrusion uprights — shows intended orientation and scale).

## Print Orientation

The plug portion prints vertically (plug axis = Z). The forked cradle branches horizontally from the plug root. This orientation places the highest-stress cross-section (plug-to-cradle transition) parallel to the print layers, which is the weakest axis for layer-adhesion in CF-reinforced filaments. **Flag for print-reviewer:** consider splitting orientation to maximize layer strength in the cantilever direction, or evaluate whether the selected CF material's inter-layer adhesion is adequate for the stated load case.

Alternative orientation: cradle face-down on bed with plug pointing up. This keeps the bending-moment axis perpendicular to layer lines (stronger) but introduces overhangs on the fork tines. At 30° off vertical (60° included), tine inner faces are within the 45° self-supporting limit — this orientation is viable and may be preferable for structural reasons. Modeler should model in a way that supports both orientations; print-reviewer should recommend final orientation after geometry analysis.

## Features

### Feature 1: Internal Plug

- **Purpose:** Anchors the holder inside the extrusion bore; resists the cantilever moment generated by the dumbbell weight.
- **Critical dimensions:**
  - Outer cross-section: 42.5 mm × 68.5 mm rectangular with 2.5 mm corner radius
  - Engagement depth: 30 mm (`plug_depth`)
  - Wall thickness: minimum 3.0 mm on all sides (CF minimum — raised from 1.2 mm PLA standard)
- **Mating interface:** Slides into extrusion ID 44.5 mm × 70.5 mm, inner corner r < 3 mm
  - Fit type: sliding / clearance (1 mm/side user-specified, 2 mm diametric gap)
  - Resulting gap: 1.0 mm on each face — easy insertion, some rattle; acceptable for a gravity-held sliding fit
- **Load case (cantilever):** Dumbbell 1–3 lb (0.45–1.36 kg) × 9.81 m/s² ≈ 4.4–13.3 N applied at the V-seat center, approximately 70 mm from the extrusion face (cradle_reach). Bending moment at plug root: 4.4–13.3 N × (70 + 8) mm = roughly 340–1040 N·mm. CF-reinforced material at 30 mm engagement depth is shorter than the original 80 mm proposal — see resolved decisions note. Print-reviewer must confirm that wall cross-section at the root is adequate.
- **Printability note:** 30 mm tall rectangular section prints cleanly. No overhangs. No bridges. No flags.

### Feature 2: Seating Flange

- **Purpose:** Hard stop that prevents the plug from being driven deeper into the extrusion under load. Seats flush against the extrusion rim face, providing a consistent mounting datum.
- **Critical dimensions:**
  - Thickness: 8 mm
  - Radial overhang past plug face: 10 mm all sides
  - Outer envelope: 62.5 mm × 88.5 mm (narrow × wide), corner radius 7 mm (clears extrusion OD corner r 6 mm with 1 mm margin)
- **Mating interface:** Rests on extrusion outer face (51.0 mm × 76.5 mm, outer r = 6 mm). No mechanical attachment — gravity keeps it seated.
- **Printability note:** Flange prints as a flat horizontal layer — no overhangs. At 8 mm thick it is well above the minimum 3 mm (4-layer floor) threshold.

### Feature 3: Fork Transition / Stem

- **Purpose:** Structural transition from the rectangular plug/flange geometry to the forked cradle tines. This is the highest-stress region — bending moment is maximum here.
- **Critical dimensions:** Stem cross-section should be generous (suggest ≥ 15 mm × 15 mm solid or heavily-walled hollow cross-section). Modeler has discretion; flag for geometry-analysis review.
- **Printability note:** Transition from rectangular to forked profile is complex geometry — Fusion loft/sweep is well-suited. No specific overhang concern at the stem; concern is at the tine branch point.

### Feature 4: Cradle Fork Tines — Curved U-Saddle

- **Purpose:** The two tines form a curved saddle that cradles the dumbbell shaft. The shaft (D 46 mm, r 23 mm) rests in the saddle under gravity. The bells (D 111 mm) are too large to pass through the fork gap and act as axial stops on both sides.
- **Seat profile:** Smooth continuous curve — NOT a straight V. The base of the seat is a circular arc of radius 23 mm (matching the shaft radius) that cradles the shaft conformally. The arc transitions tangentially into two flared arms that diverge at 30° off vertical each (60° included). There is no sharp vertex at the throat. The shaft drops into the curved U-saddle and the conforming geometry distributes contact across the arc rather than two point contacts.
- **Critical dimensions:**
  - Saddle arc radius: 23 mm (= shaft radius; snug conforming fit, no tolerance needed — gravity-only contact)
  - Fork spread angle: 60° included (30° per arm off vertical)
  - Cradle reach: 70 mm from extrusion face (plug face side of flange) to seat arc centerline
  - Fork opening at tine tips: must be wider than 46 mm so the shaft can drop in from above — with 30° flare, opening increases as tines extend; this is met by geometry
  - Tine wall thickness: ≥ 3 mm minimum (CF brittleness requirement)
  - Tine length from branch point to tip: sufficient to provide a retention lip above shaft center; suggest tine tip extends ≥ 20 mm above saddle arc centerline (so shaft cannot bounce clear)
- **Mating interface:** Shaft rests conformally in arc saddle under gravity only. Bell flanks each side of the fork — no dimension required for this interface (bells are simply too large to pass through).
- **Printability note:** At 30° per arm off vertical, the inner tine faces fall within the 45° self-supporting limit — no support required on inner faces (see resolved decisions). Tine tips should be checked for minimum wall thickness.

## Material & Tolerances

- **Material:** Carbon-fiber-reinforced filament (CF-PLA, CF-PA, or CF-PETG) on the Bambu Lab X1C.
- **CF-specific tolerance note:** CF-reinforced filaments typically shrink less than unfilled PLA. The project's standard `fdm-pla.scad` tolerance table (press fit −0.15, clearance +0.25, sliding +0.35 mm/side) is calibrated for plain PLA. For CF materials, clearances may run tight by 0.05–0.15 mm. The 1 mm/side plug clearance is generous enough to absorb this variation, but a test-pocket print is strongly recommended before printing the full part.
- **CF brittleness:** CF-reinforced filaments have higher stiffness but lower impact toughness than unfilled filaments, and inter-layer adhesion can be weaker. Minimum wall thickness for all structural features: 3.0 mm (vs. 1.2 mm PLA standard).
- **Fit types used:**
  - Plug-to-extrusion: sliding/clearance, 1 mm/side (user-specified, conservative, test-print-pinned)
  - Shaft-in-saddle: gravity cradle, conforming arc contact — no mechanical fit tolerance

## Constraints

- Build volume: 256 × 256 × 256 mm (Bambu X1C). Bounding box is 88.5 × 108 × 62.5 mm — well within build volume.
- Nozzle: 0.4 mm. Minimum functional wall for CF: 3.0 mm (≥ 7 perimeters). Spec enforces this on all load-bearing walls.
- Single-piece part, no assembly, no fasteners, no inserts.
- Gravity-held mount — no straps, clamps, or adhesive. User has confirmed gravity + 30 mm plug engagement is acceptable. No retention feature required.

## Resolved Decisions

All questions from the initial spec are now closed:

1. **Flange dimensions:** 8 mm thick × 10 mm radial overhang on all sides. User-confirmed. Rationale: beefed up from the initial 5/5 suggestion to seat firmly against the rim and resist the cantilever moment.

2. **Cradle reach:** 70 mm from plug face to V-seat centerline. User-confirmed.

3. **Seat profile and throat gap:** No straight V. Smooth continuous curve: circular saddle arc R23 mm (conforming to shaft) at the base, transitioning tangentially into the flared tines. No throat gap dimension needed — the conforming arc contacts the shaft continuously.

4. **Fork spread angle:** 60° included (30° per arm off vertical). User-confirmed.

5. **Mount location:** Clear open end of the extrusion bore. User-confirmed bore is unobstructed — plug can engage fully without clearing internal end-cap features.

6. **Vibration retention:** Gravity only + deep engagement. No setscrew, friction pad, or snap tab. User-confirmed.

7. **Plug engagement depth:** 30 mm. User-confirmed. This is shorter than the initial 80 mm proposal. Trade: shorter engagement reduces the moment-resisting lever arm inside the bore. The bending moment at the root (~340–1040 N·mm) must be resisted by 30 mm of plug wall contact against the extrusion ID. CF-reinforced filament and the user's acceptance of this trade are on record. Print-reviewer must confirm wall section adequacy.

## v3 Amendment (2026-05-08) — External Sleeve + Lowered Fork

### Why v3

Two corrections to the v1/v2 design baseline:

1. **Shaft orientation (corrected from v1).** The shaft hangs **parallel** to the extrusion (along the plug-axis direction), not perpendicular. The dumbbell hangs in front of the post, vertical-along-the-rail. The saddle profile is in a plane perpendicular to the shaft (i.e. perpendicular to the plug axis); the shaft passes through a slot cut through the holder.
2. **External sleeve added; fork lowered.** A sleeve wraps the extrusion's outside in addition to the internal plug, providing dual-engagement (inside + outside the rail) for greater stiffness against the cantilever moment. The fork is moved down ~20 mm below the rail-top face so that the dumbbell load is applied at the *middle* of the engaged region (plug + sleeve) rather than at the top — this distributes stress across both engagements and reduces stress concentration at the plug root.

### v3 coordinate frame

Same convention as v2:
- **X** — wide flange axis (lateral, ±44.25 mm)
- **Y** — perpendicular to plug axis = cradle-extension direction. +Y is "outboard" / in front of the rail.
- **Z** — plug axis = rail axis. Plug enters in −Z. Origin at flange-front face (= rail-mating datum, at Z = 0 in this amendment's convention).

(Note: the v2 modeling-report used origin at flange-back face Z = −8. This amendment uses flange-front Z = 0 to match the rail-datum convention. Modeler should pick whichever is convenient; the geometry is the same.)

### Feature 5: External Sleeve (NEW in v3)

- **Purpose:** Wraps the rail's outer surface to provide a second engagement point. With the plug (inside the rail) and the sleeve (outside the rail) both engaged, the holder is constrained against both axial slip and rotational tip-out by two opposing surfaces — much stiffer than plug-only engagement.
- **Critical dimensions:**
  - Outer envelope: 88.5 mm × 62.5 mm (matches flange OD), corner radius 7 mm
  - Inner cavity: 78.5 mm × 53.0 mm (rail OD + 2 × 1 mm clearance), corner radius 7 mm
  - Wall thickness: 5.0 mm (wide axis), 4.75 mm (narrow axis)
  - Length below flange-front face: 30 mm (matches `plug_depth`)
- **Mating interface:** Slides over the rail's outer surface (51.0 × 76.5 mm with 6 mm corner r). 1 mm/side clearance (sliding fit).
- **Local beef-up at fork junction:** In the Z range of the fork plate (saddle_centerline_z ± fork_thickness_z/2), the +Y portion of the sleeve wall is replaced by the fork plate material extending out from Y = +26.5 (sleeve inner +Y face) to Y = +110 (tine tips). The fork plate's "base" effectively IS a locally-thickened section of the sleeve +Y wall at this Z. This handles the cantilever-bending moment without thickening the rest of the sleeve.
- **Printability note:** Sleeve is essentially a hollow rectangular shell. Printed in plug-vertical orientation (sleeve walls vertical), no overhangs. In cradle-flat orientation, the sleeve becomes a perimeter wall with overhangs on the inside corners — needs evaluation.

### Updated Feature 4: Cradle Fork Tines — Saddle Slot (v3)

Replaces the v1/v2 "U-saddle the shaft drops into" with a "U-slot the shaft passes through":
- **Purpose:** Holds the shaft constrained laterally (in X) while the shaft passes through the slot in the plug-axis direction (Z). Upper bell of the dumbbell rests on the fork plate's top surface; shaft hangs vertically through the slot; lower bell hangs free below the holder.
- **Seat profile:** Same as v1/v2 in shape — R23 arc tangent to two 30°-flared arms (60° included). The profile now lives in the **horizontal plane** (perpendicular to the plug/shaft axis Z) instead of the vertical plane.
- **Critical dimensions:**
  - Saddle arc radius: 23 mm (= shaft radius, conforming fit)
  - Fork spread angle: 60° included (30° per arm)
  - Saddle centerline position: (X = 0, Y = +90 mm, Z = saddle_centerline_z)
  - Saddle centerline Z: Z = −(fork_offset_below_top + flange_thickness) measured from flange-top — i.e. 20 mm below the rail-top face = Z = −20 (with origin at flange-front). With the v2-style origin at flange-back, saddle Z = −28.
  - Slot thickness (= fork plate Z thickness = shaft bearing length): 12 mm
  - Tine tips: at Y = +110 (= saddle centerline + 20 mm), open at the +Y plate edge so the shaft can be slid in laterally.
- **Mating interface:** Shaft passes through the slot vertically. Bell rests on plate top under gravity. No mechanical retention against +Y pull-out (intentional — same gravity-only philosophy as earlier versions).
- **Insertion procedure:** Lift the dumbbell, slide the shaft sideways into the slot from the +Y open end until it seats at the arc center. Release.

### v3 load-case summary

- Same dumbbell mass: 0.45–1.36 kg → 4.4–13.3 N at saddle.
- Cantilever lever arm in Y: 90 mm (from rail center).
- Bending moment about plug-fixation horizontal axis: 4.4–13.3 N × 90 mm = **400–1200 N·mm**. (Up from ~340–1040 N·mm in v1/v2 because cradle reach increased from 70 to 90 mm.)
- This moment is now resisted by **plug + sleeve in opposition** rather than plug alone. The plug pushes against the −Y interior wall of the rail at its top, the sleeve pushes against the +Y exterior wall of the rail at its bottom (or vice-versa depending on direction of tip-out). The effective lever arm of the rail-holder reaction couple is now ≈ `plug_depth + sleeve_length = 60 mm` (twice the v1/v2 value of 30 mm), so the reaction force at each engagement is roughly half of v1/v2.
- Net structural improvement: the moment arm of the dumbbell is slightly longer in v3 (90 vs 70), so the input moment is ~30 % larger — but the rail-holder reaction couple has 2× the effective lever arm, so each engagement sees ~35 % less force than in v1/v2. Overall stiffness increases substantially.

- [ ] Sleeve length: 30 mm matches plug. Could be shorter (down to ~20 mm) to save material, or longer (up to ~50 mm) for more stability. **Defaulting to 30 mm; modeler may parametrize.**

### v3.2 update — fork-top buttress

User-requested: "big fillet on the top of the fork to give it strength — roll that all the way to the top edge of the block."
