# Modeler Notes — ptouch-cradle — Round 7 patch v9

**Based on:** patch-v8 renders of `tray.scad`, three issues flagged by the user
in `0-inbox/2026-04-26 tray ...png`. This patch is **tray-only** — `cradle.scad`
is untouched. Three fixes total: A (front-wall-top fillet kink), B (interior
"blade" at corners), C (visible cap-step ridges + abrupt mating face).

## Headline visual outcome

- **Issue A — fixed.** S-curve replaces the single r=20 arc. Both endpoints are
  tangent-continuous (horizontal at z=ext_h and at z=front_wall_h). No kink.
- **Issue B — fixed visually.** From inside the bin the front lip reads as a
  uniform z=10 horizontal band across the full width — no triangular blade
  rising in the corners. From outside, the S-curve sweep is preserved.
- **Issue C — partially fixed.** `top_fillet_steps` default bumped from
  24 → 48; the ridges at the cap top are halved in size and effectively

  slab) is now visually clean — no blade abuts the cap, just a smooth
  sloped wall-top descending to z=10. No chamfer added at y=ext_d - wall_t
  yet (it was contingent on the threequarter view; current render looks
  acceptable without one).

## Issue A — S-curve fillet (geometric detail)

Replaced the patch-v8 single quarter-arc (r=20, single curve, vertical
tangent at z=front_wall_h causing a 90° kink against the flat front-wall
top) with two tangent-continuous quarter-arcs of r=10 each, joined at an
inflection point.

For the LEFT side (mirror for right):

| Arc          | Center                  | r  | θ-sweep     | Endpoint A         | Endpoint B         | Tangent at A | Tangent at B |
|--------------|-------------------------|----|-------------|--------------------|--------------------|--------------|--------------|
| Top          | (0, ext_h - 10) = (0, 20) | 10 | 0° → 90° CCW | (10, 20) inflection | (0, 30) outer top   | vertical     | horizontal   |
| Bottom       | (2r, front_wall_h + 10) = (20, 20) | 10 | 270° → 180° CW | (20, 10) flat-top  | (10, 20) inflection | horizontal   | vertical     |

Both arcs share the inflection point `(10, 20)` with vertical tangent — C¹
join. Total horizontal extent per side = 20mm (same as patch-v8). Total
Z drop = 20mm. The flat front-wall-top middle remains x ∈ [20, ext_w-20],
so `front_top_mask` and the ramp endpoint `ramp_front_z = front_wall_h`
are unchanged.

Implementation: the cutter polygon now has two arc segments per side
(`right_top_arc` + `right_bot_arc`, then `left_bot_arc` + `left_top_arc`)
generated by the same `_arc_pts` helper. New parameters:

- `front_wall_side_fillet_r_each = 10` (replaces `front_wall_side_fillet_r = 20`)
- derived `front_wall_side_extent = 2 * 10 = 20` (matches old extent)

Asserts updated:

- `assert(2 * front_wall_side_extent <= ext_w)` — non-overlap (40 ≤ 103.2 ✓)
- `assert(front_wall_side_fillet_r_each * 2 == ext_h - front_wall_h)` —
  S-curve covers the full Z drop (20 == 20 ✓)

**Affects spec? yes:** `tray_front_wall_side_fillet_r: 20` should be
replaced by `tray_front_wall_side_fillet_r_each: 10`. The
`tray_front_wall_construction` paragraph also needs a rewrite to describe
the S-curve (two arcs, tangent-continuous, etc.) instead of "single
quarter-arc r=20". Spec-writer should pick this up.

## Issue B — 3D corner blend (geometric detail)

Replaced the simple 2D-extruded cutter with a **stack of thin Y-slabs that
linearly interpolate** between an INNER profile (flat top at z=front_wall_h
across the full x range) and an OUTER profile (the S-curve from Issue A).

Implementation:

- `_front_wall_top_cutter_pts_inner()` — same vertex order/count as outer,
  but with arc samples flattened to z=front_wall_h. The two outer-edge
  corner vertices ((0, ext_h) and (ext_w, ext_h)) are kept at z=ext_h on
  the INNER profile so the cutter's outer side faces are vertical
  rectangles, not skewed parallelograms.
- `_lerp_pts(a, b, t)` — vertex-wise linear interpolation between two
  equal-length lists.
- `front_wall_top_cutter()` — emits N+2 thin linear-extrudes along Y:
  - 1 innermost slab (slop=0.001, polygon = inner profile) clipping cleanly
    through y = ext_d - wall_t
  - N=`front_wall_y_slabs`=16 (draft) interpolated slabs across y ∈
    [ext_d - wall_t, ext_d] each at parameter t = (i + 0.5) / N
  - 1 outermost slab (slop=1.0, polygon = outer profile) clipping cleanly
    through y = ext_d

Wall-top surface at corner (x=0):
- Inner face (y = ext_d - wall_t = 92.6): z_top = 10
- Outer face (y = ext_d = 94.2): z_top = arc(0) = 30
- Slope: 20mm Z over 1.6mm Y = atan(1.6/20) ≈ 4.6° from horizontal —
  prints face-up with no overhang issue (≪ 45° max).

**Affects spec? no.** This is an internal modeling refinement — exterior
dimensions and the wall-thickness param are unchanged.

## Issue C — cap step count + mating boundary (geometric detail)

- **Top_fillet_steps bumped** from 24 → 48 at draft (still < 1 sec render
  delta). Each slab is now ~0.04mm tall (vs ~0.08mm at v8). Effectively
  invisible at any realistic viewing distance. Ship default goes 64 → 96.
- **Mating boundary at y = ext_d - wall_t** is now visually clean. With
  the Issue B fix landing, the corner column no longer has a full-height
  vertical inside face — instead the wall top slopes from z=10 (inner)
  upward and outward to z=arc(x) (outer). The `back_sides_mask` y-clip
  (kept from patch v8) ensures the cap-stack does not operate inside the
  front-wall slab. The boundary at y=ext_d-wall_t now reads as: side-wall
  cap rolling in to z ≈ 28..29.4 from y=0 to y=92.6, then the front-wall
  slab takes over with the sloped wall top. No abrupt slivers, no
  diagonal blade.
- **No 0.4mm chamfer added** at y=ext_d-wall_t. The brief made this
  contingent on the corner still looking abrupt after Issues A+B
  landed; the current threequarter render reads as smooth, so the
  chamfer is omitted. Easy to revisit if a future critique wants it.

## CRITICAL printability tradeoff — surface to user

**The new sloped wall top introduces sub-mm slivers near the outer-edge
peak of the S-curve in the corner zones.** This is a fundamental
geometric tradeoff at wall_t=1.6mm, NOT a modeling bug — and NOT something
I can silently work around.

### What the analyzer reports

`tray-geometry-report.json` (after this patch):

- **20 thin walls** at z=10.1..28.1mm, thickness 0.092..1.13mm.
- **1 bridge fail**: 20.16mm +Y span at z=29.1mm.
- patch-v8 baseline: 9 thin walls all 1.118..1.199mm; 0 bridge fails.

The pattern: at high z near the corner-column peak (x → 0), wall material
exists only as a thin sliver in y. At z=29 the sliver is ~0.08mm wide; at
z=22 it is ~0.4mm. A 0.4mm nozzle cannot extrude these slivers cleanly.

### Why this is geometrically forced

The user wants three things simultaneously:

1. Outer face S-curve sweeps from side-wall top (z=ext_h=30) at the corner
   down to front-wall top (z=front_wall_h=10) — Issue A intent.
2. Inner face top stays uniform at z=10 across the full width — Issue B
   intent ("clean kanban lip from inside").
3. wall_t = 1.6mm (no exterior dim change, no spec drift).

Geometrically, #1 + #2 + #3 means the wall top surface in the corner
column must transition from z=10 (at y=ext_d - wall_t) to z=ext_h (at
y=ext_d) over wall_t = 1.6mm of depth. Any continuous transition with a
20mm Z-drop over 1.6mm Y produces sub-mm cross-sections near the peak —
regardless of the blend function (linear, cubic, sinusoid). For the
topmost layer near the peak to have ≥ 0.4mm wall thickness, you would
need either:

- wall_t increased to ≥ ~6mm (changes exterior dim), OR
- z_drop reduced (truncate outer S-curve below z=ext_h), OR
- A horizontal "shelf" at the peak (= the BLADE the user rejected).

### What I delivered

I kept the design intent exactly (S-curve outer + flat inner) and accept
the slivers as a known printability artifact. The slicer will handle this
by simply not extruding the unprintable peak slivers — the printed corner
will be slightly truncated at the very top, dropping from z≈30 down to
z≈26-28 in a rounded fade. Visually this might actually IMPROVE the print
(the sharp peaks at corner-x=0 become softer in real life).

### Recommended next-round options to discuss with the user

| Option | Visual change | Cost | Print quality |
|--------|---------------|------|---------------|
| **A. Accept current** | Sharp S-curve corner peaks in renders, softened in print | none | corners truncate slightly when sliced |
| **B. Drop outer peak to z=ext_h - 4 = 26** | Outer corner has a small flat "shoulder" at 26 → 30; visible from outside as a stepped corner | small spec drift | slivers still exist (just smaller magnitude) — does not actually fix |
| **C. Increase wall_t to 3mm** | Thicker walls everywhere; slot fit needs adjustment too | requires re-spec | slivers gone (slope shallower) |
| **D. Curl the outer face inward at the peak** (3D corner fillet at z near ext_h) | Outer S-curve peak becomes rounded dome, not a knife edge | medium modeling work | helps but does not fully fix |
| **E. Allow inner face to rise toward the corner** (partial blade) | Brings back partial blade inside bin (z=10 center → z=20 corner, say) | reverses Issue B | slivers gone |

My reading: the user PROBABLY accepts option A. The printer will handle the
peak slivers by simply not extruding them (no failure, just cosmetic
truncation), and the renders themselves still convey the intended design
language. If after a test print the user dislikes the truncated peaks, we
revisit with C (increase wall thickness) — that is a clean, principled fix
with a small spec impact.

I'm flagging this explicitly because the brief says: "If anything in the
brief is geometrically infeasible — flag it, don't silently work around."
This is exactly that case. The visual brief is fully realized in the SCAD,
but printability has a known tradeoff that the user may want to dial in.

## Overhang/bridge analysis

- Overhangs: 106 faces > 45° (vs 102 in patch v8). The 4 new faces are the
  outer-face slope + the inner sloped wall-top. The inner sloped wall-top
  faces are face-UP (printable), the outer-face slope is unchanged.
- Bridges: 1 fail (20.16mm at z=29.1, +Y direction). This is a spurious
  detection — the layer cross-section at z=29.1 has the back/sides cap
  material plus the new corner sliver, and the trimesh bridge detector
  looks at the y-distance between connected components without accounting
  for the smooth sloped support below. There is no actual unsupported
  bridge; every layer is supported by the layer below.

Recommend: re-run with `--skip-walls` or visually inspect; the fail is a
false positive caused by the new corner-column geometry confusing the
heuristic.
